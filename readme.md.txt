Project Overview
This repository contains my Digital Literacy Project completed as part of the CSE0001 course at VIT Bhopal. The main purpose of this project was to understand how digital tools are used in everyday academic and professional environments, and how students can use them responsibly.

In today"s world , Digital literacy is not only limited to having knowledge about digital media and their tools butalso having knowledge to imply digital tools in your daily digital task such as assignments, tasks and presentation. By this project I have learbnt what digital literacy really is. I use all given digital tools effectively in my project.

Description of Tasks
Task 1 – Digital Literacy Infographic
In this task, I created an infographic using Canva to explain the concept of digital literacy. The design included key points such as the importance of digital skills, useful tools for students and safe internet practices. While designing, I focused on keeping the content simple and visually clear so that it can be easily understood. This task helped me improve my ability to present information in a structured and creative way.

Task 2 – Digital Portfolio
For this task, I developed my digital presence by creating profiles on platforms like GitHub and LinkedIn. GitHub allows me to store and manage my projects, while LinkedIn helps in building a professional identity, also i9 set up mu identity on kaggle. Setting up these profiles made me realize the importance of maintaining a clean and professional online image, especially for future internships and career opportunities.

Task 3 – Coding & Collaboration Platforms
In this task, I explored coding practice platforms and collaboration tools. I completed a beginner-level problem on HackerRank, which helped me understand logical thinking and problem-solving. I also created a Google Form as a Digital Literacy Quiz and viewed responses through Google Sheets. This task showed how online tools can be used effectively for learning and data collection.

Task 4 – Email Etiquette & Communication
This task focused on professional communication. I wrote two formal emails—one to a professor and another to an internship coordinator. I learned how to structure emails properly, use a respectful tone, and avoid informal language. Additionally, I created a checklist of do’s and don’ts for social media usage, which highlights responsible digital behavior.

Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness
In the final task, I studied a cybercrime scenario and created a prevention checklist. This helped me understand how online frauds like phishing or payment scams occur and how easily users can become targets. I also learned simple preventive measures such as verifying links, protecting personal information, and using secure passwords.
